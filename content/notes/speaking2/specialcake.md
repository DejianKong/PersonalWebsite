 ## PART 2
**Question**

Describe a special cake you received from others

You should say:

when it happened

where it happened

who you got the cake from

and explain why it is a special cake.

**Answer**

The special cake I'm going to describe is one I received from my roommates.

It happened on February 23rd, which was my birthday a few months ago. The whole thing took place in my university dormitory. I'm a postgraduate student at Nanjing University, so I live in the campus dormitory.

I got this cake from my three roommates. We have been close friends since the start of the school year.

This cake is really special for several reasons. First of all, it was handmade instead of being bought from a bakery shop. I knew my roommates had spent a lot of time and effort making it by themselves. Even though it didn't look perfectly decorated, it tasted really delicious. What matters most is their sincerity and warm birthday wishes.

Besides, they drew many classic film characters on the cake. I'm a huge fan of superhero movies like Spider-Man and Iron Man, and they knew my hobby well. This little personal touch made the cake feel extra meaningful.

I was really moved by their thoughtfulness. That's definitely the most special cake I have ever received, and I will remember this warm moment for a long time.

 ## PART 3
1. **In your country, do people nowadays cook at home as frequently as people did in the past?**

Actually, I believe that people nowadays don't cook at home as frequently as people did in the past. One of the main reasons is that lots of people have full-time jobs. They are busy with their work and cannot spare any time to cook at home, so they usually choose to have meals outside. Apart from that, many people earn a good salary and have well-paid jobs. They can afford to eat at restaurants easily, so they seldom cook at home in their daily life.

2. **What do you think of people using their mobile phones during a meal?**

Actually, I think it is a negative development. Having meals is a perfect chance to put away our phones and escape from the digital world. We can fully immerse ourselves in the moment and enjoy the food we have. Apart from that, if you dine with others, staring at your phone is really rude and impolite. People should communicate with each other while eating, because it is a wonderful way to build and strengthen close relationships with family and friends.

3. **What are the differences between special food in China and other countries?**

I think the main difference is the way people cook food. Take traditional Cantonese cuisine as an example. People living in Cantonese areas don't use too many heavy ingredients when they cook. They prefer simple cooking methods like steaming or stir-frying. These simple ways can better preserve the original flavour and freshness of the food. Generally speaking, Cantonese food tastes quite light, simple and healthy compared with rich-flavoured food in many other countries.

4. **Is there any food in your country that is eaten in special times or on special occasions?**

Yes, of course. Chinese people usually eat special food on traditional special occasions. Take the Mid-Autumn Festival as an example. We usually have mooncakes, which are a special traditional Chinese dessert. There are many different mooncake fillings, such as salted egg yolk, red bean paste and pork. They have a crispy crust, and some flavours taste a little oily and sweet. Mooncakes have become an essential food for family reunion on this traditional festival.

5. **Why are some people willing to spend a lot of money on meals on special dates?**

I think it is mainly because having meals on special days is a great way to celebrate with others. Take the Spring Festival as an example. Many Chinese families are willing to spend a lot of money on family feasts. They think it is a precious chance to get together and celebrate special occasions. They order various delicious dishes to satisfy their appetite, which leaves them wonderful memories and happy experiences with their loved ones.

6. **Do you think it is good to communicate when eating with your family?**

Yes, of course. In China, people really enjoy communicating with family members over meals. One main reason is that family members often work or study in different cities. When they finally gather together for family dinners, they like to share their daily life, such as work experience and their feelings about study. It is a great way to know what is happening in each other's life and provide emotional support for the whole family.
